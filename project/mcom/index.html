Macys Reimagined

Improve key functionalities of the iPhone apps for Macys and Bloomingdales. Focusing on users' personality and behavior to provide contextual shopping experiences.

My Role

Discovered new opportunities in the mobile retail space through market research, user interviews, and on-site experiments. To evaluate our learnings, we conducted stakeholder meetings and user testing sessions. Designed the new features in high-fidelity prototypes and detailed documentation for the hand-off. I worked with Pauly Ting, a fellow UX designer on this project

01.

Design Process

Understand & Relate
Started by empathizing with the end users to effectively understand the context and the human side of the story. Researched most modern m-commerce solutions and real-world retail experiences.
Research & Analyze
Created user-persona and user-story work-flows by conducting in-person interviews with subject matter experts and current users. Managed to quickly distill and analyze key themes to drive select functionalities.
Prototype & Iterate
Tested and iterated all ideas through interactive mobile prototypes. We also hosted multiple educational workshops on modern mobile practices and design team structures.

Conclusion

The design strategy and prototypes was very well received by Macy's stakeholders and we were subsequently engaged into the next stage of the project. It also inspired an array of new functionalities within the product road-map for the next few releases.

Learnings

By conducting stakeholder interviews, additional line-items were identified for the project scope. Its very important to plan back-up resources for high profile projects to avoid any adjustments to the overall timeline. Thanks to Pauly Ting.
